How to calculate percentage off

A percentage off calculation tells you how much money you save and what the final sale price will be after a discount. To find the sale price, multiply the original price by the discount rate as a decimal to get the savings, then subtract the savings from the original price. For example, 25 percent off a $120 item means multiplying 120 by 0.25 to get $30 in savings, then subtracting $30 to get a sale price of $90.

If you already know the original price and the sale price, you can work backward to find the percentage off. Subtract the sale price from the original price to get the savings amount, then divide the savings by the original price and multiply by 100. This is useful when a store only shows the reduced price and you want to know how strong the discount really is.

Formula explanation

There are two common formulas. First, savings = original price × discount percentage. Second, sale price = original price - savings. To reverse the problem, use percent off = savings ÷ original price × 100. These formulas work for clothing, electronics, groceries, subscriptions, services, and almost any other shopping situation.

A calculator can be especially helpful when comparing several sale items at once. A 30 percent discount on one product may still cost more than a 15 percent discount on a cheaper item. Looking at both the final price and the savings amount gives a clearer picture than percentages alone.

Shopping discount examples

Imagine a jacket priced at $200 with a 40 percent discount. The savings are $80, so the sale price is $120. Now imagine a second item that drops from $95 to $76. The savings are $19, which means the discount is 20 percent. These comparisons show why it helps to calculate both directions, either from original price and percent off, or from original price and sale price.

During seasonal sales, stacking coupons, taxes, and shipping can also affect the true value of a deal. A percentage off calculator gives you a quick starting point so you can compare offers more confidently before buying.

Frequently asked questions

How do I find the sale price after a discount?

Multiply the original price by the discount rate to find savings, then subtract that amount from the original price.

How do I find the discount percent from two prices?

Subtract the sale price from the original price, divide by the original price, and multiply by 100.

Does percentage off include tax?

Usually no. Discounts are normally applied before tax, but store policies can vary.

Can I use this for coupons and promo codes?

Yes. Any price reduction that behaves like a percentage discount can be estimated with this tool.
