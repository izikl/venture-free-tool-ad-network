Standard tip percentages

Tipping customs vary by country, city, and service type, but many people use a simple range to decide on gratuity. In restaurants, 15 percent is often considered a basic tip for standard service, 18 percent is common for good service, and 20 percent or more is often used for excellent service. For bars, taxis, delivery, salons, and ride services, people usually choose a flat amount or a percentage that reflects the quality of service and local expectations.

Preset buttons make this easier because you can compare several common tip rates without manual math. If you want to reward outstanding service, choosing 20 percent or 25 percent can show appreciation clearly. If you are working with a tight budget, a calculator can help you see the exact difference between each option before you pay.

Tipping guide by service type

Restaurants often receive the highest percentage tips because table service can be time intensive. Taxi or rideshare tips are often around 10 to 20 percent, depending on distance, help with bags, and overall experience. Food delivery tips can depend on weather, distance, order size, and how difficult the delivery was. Hair stylists, barbers, hotel staff, and spa workers also commonly receive tips. When in doubt, check local customs, since tipping expectations differ widely around the world.

How to calculate a tip manually

To calculate a tip by hand, multiply the bill amount by the tip percentage as a decimal. For example, for an $80 bill and an 18 percent tip, multiply 80 by 0.18 to get $14.40. Add that to the bill to get the total of $94.40. If you are splitting the bill, divide the total by the number of people. To find the tip per person, divide the tip amount by the same number of people. This tool performs all of those steps automatically, which is especially helpful when the bill includes tax, multiple diners, or a custom percentage.

Frequently asked questions

Should I tip before or after tax?

Practices vary. Many people tip based on the pre-tax subtotal, while others tip on the full total for simplicity.

How much should I tip for great service?

Many people choose 20 percent or more when service is excellent, especially in places where tipping is standard.

Can I use this for group dinners?

Yes. Enter the number of people to split the total and the tip evenly across the group.

What if a service charge is already included?

If gratuity is already added, you may not need an extra tip unless you want to leave more.
