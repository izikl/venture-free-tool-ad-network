How to calculate running pace

Running pace is the time it takes you to cover a set distance, usually expressed in minutes per mile or minutes per kilometer. To calculate your pace, divide your total run time (in minutes) by the distance covered. For example, if you ran 3 miles in 30 minutes, your pace is 10 minutes per mile. Runners in countries using the metric system typically express pace as minutes per kilometer instead.

To find your finish time for a race, multiply your pace by the race distance. A 10:00 per mile pace over a 26.22-mile marathon gives a finish time of 4 hours 21 minutes and 43 seconds. This calculator handles the conversions and time arithmetic automatically, including the correct distances for standard race formats.

Converting between pace and speed

Pace and speed express the same thing in different ways. Pace is time per distance; speed is distance per time. To convert a 10:00 per mile pace to miles per hour, divide 60 by 10, giving 6 mph. To convert 5 mph to a pace, divide 60 by 5, giving 12:00 per mile. For kilometers, divide 60 by your pace in minutes per km to get km/h, or divide 60 by your speed in km/h to get your pace in minutes per km.

Treadmill speeds are typically expressed in mph or km/h, so converting between pace and speed is useful when setting treadmill intensity. A common outdoor pace of 10:00 per mile corresponds to a treadmill setting of 6.0 mph.

Pacing strategies for races

Negative splitting means running the second half of a race faster than the first half. It is generally considered the most efficient pacing strategy for long distances because it prevents burning out early. Even splitting means maintaining a consistent pace throughout. Positive splitting means starting faster and slowing down, which often results in significantly slower total times due to glycogen depletion and mental fatigue in the later miles.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good marathon pace?

A sub-4-hour marathon (9:09 per mile) is a common benchmark for recreational runners. Sub-3:30 (8:01 per mile) is solid for regular runners. Elite amateur runners target sub-3:00 (6:52 per mile). World-class runners finish under 2:10.

How do I calculate my 5K pace?

Enter your 5K finish time in the Find Pace tab. For example, a 30-minute 5K gives a pace of 9:39 per mile or 6:00 per kilometer.

What is a mile in minutes on a treadmill?

A treadmill set to 6.0 mph takes 10 minutes to cover one mile. At 5.0 mph, a mile takes 12 minutes. At 7.5 mph, a mile takes 8 minutes.
